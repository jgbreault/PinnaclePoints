Definitions

A line of sight is an unobstructed path of light between two points on Earth's surface. Only lines of sight between summits with at least 1,000 m of prominence are considered. The map only shows lines of sight that are the longest for both summits involved. Ideal atmospheric visibility conditions are assumed and the curvature of the Earth, atmospheric refraction, and local topography are all taken into account. The longest line of sight on Earth is 544.6 km long.

Prominence is the minimum vertical distance one must descend to reach a higher point.

Atmospheric refraction is the bending of light in the atmosphere due to varying temperatures and pressures.

Local topography is the specific shape of the terrain surrounding a point.
